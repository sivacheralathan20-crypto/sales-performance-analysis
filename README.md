# sales-performance-analysis
A comprehensive data analytics project analyzing Superstore sales using Python, SQL, Excel, and Power BI to generate business insights.[project01.ipynb](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/29660891/project01.ipynb)


## Project Overview
This project analyzes Superstore retail sales data to uncover business insights related to customer behavior, product performance, regional sales, shipping efficiency, and seasonal trends.

The goal is to help businesses make better data-driven decisions.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze customer contribution to revenue
- Identify top-performing product categories
- Understand regional sales performance
- Study customer segments
- Analyze shipping efficiency
- Detect seasonal demand patterns
- Evaluate product concentration risk

---

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook / Google Colab

---

## Dataset Information
The dataset contains **9800 sales transactions** with the following major columns:

- Order ID
- Order Date
- Ship Date
- Customer Name
- Segment
- Region
- Category
- Sub Category
- Product Name
- Sales

---

## Key Business Questions
1. Do a small number of customers contribute most revenue?
2. Which product categories perform best?
3. Which region contributes the most sales?
4. Which customer segment is most valuable?
5. Does shipping mode affect sales?
6. Are there seasonal sales trends?
7. Is revenue concentrated in a few products?

---

## Key Insights
- Top 20% customers contribute **48.29%** of total sales.
- Technology is the highest-performing category.
- West region contributes the highest sales.
- Consumer segment drives the highest revenue.
- Home Office has the highest average order value.
- Sales peak in November and December.
- Top 10 products contribute only **10.82%**, indicating healthy diversification.

---

## Business Recommendations
- Strengthen loyalty programs for top customers
- Increase focus on Technology category
- Expand in underperforming regions
- Improve inventory planning during Q4
- Optimize Second Class shipping

## Conclusion

This project explored retail sales performance across customers, products, regions, and operations.

Main conclusions:

- Revenue is strongly influenced by a small group of high-value customers.
- Technology emerged as the strongest-performing category.
- West and East regions dominate sales performance.
- Consumer segment drives volume, while Home Office delivers higher-value transactions.
- Sales show clear seasonality, with strong spikes in Q4.
- The product portfolio is well diversified, reducing dependency risk.

Overall, the business demonstrates strong growth potential, with opportunities for better customer retention, regional expansion, and operational optimization.
